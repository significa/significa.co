We already offer a generous amount of paid time off because we trust our team to rest, recharge, and take care of what matters most outside of work. Sometimes, though, life throws curveballs — and that's where unpaid leave can come in. Here's how we handle it.

## What it's for

Unpaid leave is for occasional, exceptional situations like:

- Needing more time off than your paid leave allows, often at short notice
- Personal circumstances that don't fit within regular leave categories
- Something unexpected — because we get it, life happens

There's one exception where you can request unpaid leave in advance:

Sabbaticals — if you're eligible and planning a longer break, log it in Calamari as Unpaid Leave with "Sabbatical" as the reason.

## What it's not for

Unpaid leave is not meant to:

- Stretch out a holiday or long weekend just because… vibes
- Act as a kind of unofficial extra annual leave allowance

We want to keep things fair for the whole team and sustainable for the business — so please keep this in mind when making a request. While unpaid leave doesn't incur a salary cost, it can still have an impact; it might disrupt project timelines, stretch team members, or affect productivity overall.

## How it works

All unpaid leave requests are approved through Calamari. These should be submitted no more than 2 weeks in advance of the time off, unless it's a sabbatical.

You'll need to include a short note explaining the reason. Requests will be approved (or not) based on whether they fit the guidance above.

Unpaid Leave is the only leave type that requires approval; everything else is automatically approved. We're only adding this step because there's sometimes confusion about when unpaid leave is the right option. This helps keep things transparent and fair.

## Trust and responsibility

We're not here to micromanage. This policy is built on mutual trust and good judgement — you know the deal: Maximum freedom, maximum responsibility. If you're unsure whether something fits, just check in with Catarina. We'll always aim to be flexible when it makes sense.
