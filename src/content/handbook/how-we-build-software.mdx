## K.I.S.S. is everything

At Significa, we write simple software. Achieving simplicity often requires more consideration and specification than complexity. Simple software allows for maintainability and usability for years with minimal upkeep. So remember: Keep it simple, stupid [hence: K.I.S.S.]. This principle is at the core of our engineering ethos and should always guide your decisions.

## Quality

We trust you've read about our commitment to never compromising on quality. We maintain high standards with our code, and this chapter of our Handbook will help you become familiar with those standards.

In short, we strive for exceptional code quality because anything less will come back to bite us. You might think that moving fast—delivering MVPs and early-stage projects—means we can skimp on quality, but that's a misconception. There's never an advantage in cutting corners on code quality. We must always aim to improve, and being meticulous about quality is fundamental to that.

### Tech stack

We're all about using a modern tech stack and keeping up with the latest trends. But don't worry—we're not just jumping on every new tech trend. Flexibility is crucial for us, so we carefully consider the developer experience when choosing our tools.

### Development tools

The choice of development tools is entirely up to you—remember our principle of Maximum freedom, maximum responsibility! There are no restrictions; each person can use the tools they believe work best for them. Remember that the development environment should always be replicable, making onboarding easier for anyone joining a project.

## Your source of truth

This chapter of the Handbook should be your North Star as a Significa developer — your source of truth for how we approach development and the quality we expect you to create.

Always keep an eye on software security and ensure you understand what's expected regarding software documentation. This applies whether you're working on front-end or back-end development. Our expectations regarding code reviews, continuous integration and delivery, and software versioning remain consistent.
