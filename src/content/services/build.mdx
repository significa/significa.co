## What we do.

Design and engineering are the same discipline at Significa. We don't hand off wireframes to developers or ask designers to "make it pretty" after the architecture is set. Every product decision, from information architecture to API design, from interaction patterns to database schema, is made by people who understand both sides.

## Our approach.

Designers and engineers work as a single, cross-functional team from the outset. We set up CI/CD, observability and security from the start, work in tight increments, review frequently, and release often. Design decisions are validated in code, not in static mockups. Engineering constraints inform design exploration, not block it.

## Our strategy.

### Systems architecture and planning

We start by making needs, objectives, constraints and risks explicit. Then we define the systems architecture, prioritise features and pick the right tech stack for the job.

### Design systems as shared language

Design Systems translate to code as tokens and component libraries, keeping implementation tightly aligned with design decisions. This shared language between design and engineering makes future changes faster and safer.

### Security and data modelling

API contracts, data models and permission rules are set with security and privacy in mind from the start. Where legacy systems or third parties are involved, we add an integration layer to simplify, normalise data and protect performance.

### Performance targets

We set clear, practical targets for speed and scale, page load times, API response times, accessibility scores, and design to meet them. Not just at launch but as the product grows.

### CI/CD and observability

Continuous integration, versioning and automated tests keep delivery predictable. Observability gives us the signals to act quickly when something needs attention.

### Open source foundations

We contribute to and rely on open source to speed up delivery. **Foundations** is our library of components, patterns and guides, built to be copied and adapted, keeping accessibility and performance front and centre.

## Why Significa.

Every technical decision is grounded in product thinking and user context, because the people making those decisions also shaped the design. We don't have a design department and an engineering department. We have project teams where both disciplines sit together and challenge each other. That's how you get products where the interface quality matches the code quality.
