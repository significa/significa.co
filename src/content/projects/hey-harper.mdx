Hey Harper didn't invent jewellery — they made it waterproof for life, backing every piece with a lifetime warranty. They had been nailing the craft and the sales. But as they scaled, their e-commerce couldn't keep up. That's where we came in.

In 6 months, we restructured, redesigned, and re-developed Hey Harper's e-commerce from the ground up using Shopify and Storyblok. The results speak for themselves.

But it didn't end there: we built a full-blown game on an e-commerce website. Users who play the game convert 7 times more, add to cart 10 times as much, and have an AOV 9% higher than those who don't. In the EU store alone, CVR increased by 17% on top of the growth shown above. All in all, these improvements generated around 3M€ in additional revenue for Hey Harper.

## First, the starting line.

Old Hey Harper was built entirely on Shopify templates. As the business grew, more and more apps made the shop heavy, clumsy, and slow. Performance, reliability, and crash-free sessions all impact CVR — so before touching a single pixel, we addressed the foundation.

We set up Sentry with a targeted 96% crash-free sessions to ensure observability and prompt action on reported issues. We also restructured the entire site map, simplified navigation from eight entry points down to two, and stripped out everything that didn't serve conversion.

We don't believe trapping clients with accounts will make them buy more. A good experience and a good product keep clients coming back. To build trust from the start, we made delivery times and free shipping thresholds visible on the product page, and surfaced all charges on the cart — no surprises at checkout.

Reviews were brought to the top of the product page to reinforce quality and influence purchasing decisions before users had to scroll.

## A bit of tech.

We ditched Shopify themes entirely. Instead, we use Storyblok as the source of truth for marketing content and page assembly, while relying on Shopify purely as the database for product and order management.

This dramatically improved performance and SEO — without a single SEO plugin. All pages are modular, block-based, and adaptable, enabling Hey Harper to mix and match content blocks as they see fit.

## Mobile.

All of it was designed and implemented mobile-first. iOS is by far the most used operating system, followed closely by Android. Every interaction, every layout decision, and every performance optimisation was made with mobile users as the primary audience.

## Other fun things.

We introduced "Shop the Look" — a full-screen immersive set that inspires customers to purchase more than one piece at a time, with a quick add-to-cart built in.

The "Style by You" section uses curated Instagram posts from real customers to show the product in use. Authentic, on-brand, and highly converting.

We also implemented swipeable thumbnail images and colour selection directly from the product grid — no need to enter a product detail page to see variations. Clean, fast, and frictionless.
