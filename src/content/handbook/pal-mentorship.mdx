PAL stands for **Peer-Assisted Learning** — a mentorship model built on the belief that some of the most meaningful growth happens between peers, not just from the top down.

At Significa, the PAL programme pairs team members across disciplines and seniority levels to create a consistent space for reflection, skill development, and honest conversation. It's structured enough to be useful, and flexible enough to be human.

## Why PAL?

We've always believed in the power of sharing knowledge — whether that's a designer explaining their thinking to a developer, or a senior engineer walking through a tricky architectural decision with someone earlier in their career.

PAL formalises that instinct. Instead of leaving mentorship to chance, we create intentional pairings and give them the tools to make those relationships genuinely valuable.

The goal isn't to turn everyone into a coach. It's to make sure that every person at Significa has someone in their corner — someone who's invested in their growth, not just their output.

## How it works

Each PAL pairing commits to regular one-on-one sessions. These sessions are yours to shape, but we provide structure to help you get the most out of them.

You'll find everything you need in the sub-pages here:

- **PAL library** — curated resources to bring into your sessions and deepen your practice.
- **Structuring your PAL sessions** — a practical guide to planning sessions that are focused and energising.
- **Question bank** — a library of prompts to spark meaningful conversation when you're not sure where to start.
- **Using ABCD to mentor** — how to use the ABCD performance framework as a lens for mentorship conversations.
- **Giving and asking for feedback** — how to make feedback a natural, constructive part of every session.

## Who is it for?

Everyone. Whether you're the mentor or the mentee — and sometimes you'll be both — PAL is designed to meet you where you are.

If you're earlier in your career, PAL gives you access to experience and perspective that accelerates your growth. If you're more senior, mentoring others sharpens your thinking, builds your leadership, and deepens your connection to the team.

The best PAL relationships go both ways.
