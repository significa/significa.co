After shipping Shelf, we reflected on what makes fintech design different from everything else we do. Here are the patterns that worked.

## Trust is designed, not declared

You can't slap a padlock icon on a screen and call it trustworthy. Trust in fintech is built through consistency, transparency, and reducing cognitive load at every step.

## Numbers need context

A balance of 1,847.32 means nothing without context. Is that good? Bad? Compared to what? The best fintech interfaces tell a story with numbers, not just display them.

## Mistakes must be reversible

In most apps, an undo is a nice-to-have. In fintech, it's essential. We designed every destructive action with a grace period and a clear escape hatch.

## The takeaway

Fintech design isn't about making banking look pretty. It's about removing every possible source of anxiety between a person and their money.

The work we did with Shelf reinforced something we already believed: the best design is the one you don't notice. You just feel confident.
