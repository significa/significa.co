Passport Natura 2000, PN2000 for short, empowers you to unleash your inner explorer with a unique gamified experience. As a company, we often take on low-budget projects whenever we identify with the cause. PN2000 is owned by Palombar, a non-governmental and non-profit Nature Heritage Conservation organisation whose mission is to preserve biodiversity, wilderness, forests, agricultural ecosystems, constructed rural heritage, and traditional crafting techniques.

As a certified B Corp, no other project would make more sense.

## What is PN2000?

In a nutshell, it is a digital passport for your adventure. As you discover the world's largest network of protected areas, Natura 2000, you'll find QR codes neatly blended into nature that you can scan to collect stamps.

As you collect said stamps and fill in your passport, locations are unlocked, and further information is made available in the app. Naturally — pun intended — the goal is to collect as many stamps as possible, and for that matter, there's a leaderboard.

## Your passport. Your stamps.

Just like a traditional passport, the more stamps you get, the more places you have been to. This one is split into two zones (for now): Douro International and Santo Adrião Mines — each with their own separate set of collectible stamps.

We wanted to make the stamps more than just a piece of the interface — more than your average badge. We wanted them to be an integral part of the experience, one added element of the gamified journey, a beautiful piece of art that would make you want to unveil more and collect further.

This is when illustrator Mariana Rio came in. She created all the stamps and illustrations, and they fit in perfectly. Each stamp is a beautiful illustration that matches the others and exponentiates the app's look and feel, turning it into a perfect blend of interface and illustration.

<MediaImage
  src="https://assets.significa.co/website/test-TKzoOc.png"

We wanted the user to feel engaged with their passport, be proud of it, and want to keep stamping it further. At the same time, we didn't want to overdesign it or overuse graphic elements that would create a heavier interface. Instead, we wanted it to live off the illustration and the collectibles rather than the interface itself.

## Scanning the codes.

You probably know how to scan a QR code, so we won't waste your time explaining how it works. But we want to show you what happens when you do.

We wanted to create a dramatic interaction that could build anticipation and emphasise the act of collecting a stamp. To make it meaningful, all the details of the animation were carefully thought through — from its motion to its duration. Everything was meticulously refined to make it a grand moment.

## Location unlocked.

Each location has a designated stamp. At first, they won't be there — neither on your passport nor the region detail page. On both pages, there are empty states for uncollected stamps.

<MediaImage
  src="https://assets.significa.co/website/test-TKzoOc.png"
  alt="Location detail page showing locked and unlocked states as stamps are collected"
  width={1920}
  height={1080}
  caption="Empty states give way to stamps and richer content as you explore"
/>

As you scan QR codes, empty states are replaced with stamps and more in-depth content. This is all part of the gamified experience we set ourselves to assemble — designed to create a sense of urgency and curiosity around stamping your passport.

## The treasure map.

One cannot simply set off and start searching for QR codes. The Natura 2000 area is huge, and finding them single-handedly is quite the challenge. So we designed a map.

<MediaImage
  src="https://assets.significa.co/website/test-TKzoOc.png"
  alt="Interactive map showing QR code locations with pinchable clusters and location pins"
  width={1920}
  height={1080}
  caption="A pinchable map — clusters break into precise location pins as you zoom in"
/>

This map contains all the available QR code locations and helps you navigate to them. It is pinchable, and as you zoom in, clusters of locations dissipate in favour of more granular ones until you can pinpoint an exact location pin.

Initially, all the pins are in an empty state. As QR codes are scanned and stamps are collected, they change into a "completed" state. Tapping a location pin opens a widget from below, letting you navigate to the location detail page.

## Offline? No problem.

The app was also built to work offline. Most of the time, PN2000 is used at remote locations — likely with no signal — where there's only you, your phone, and the wilderness.

When users open the app for the first time, it triggers a download of all written content. Not the images, though, as that would take a considerable toll on phone storage. In offline mode, you don't get to experience the beauty of the photography, the stamps, or the illustrations — which is a shame. But when you're on-site, just enjoy the view instead.

Once a location is unlocked, all the written content remains accessible regardless of connectivity. Everything you need, right when you need it.
