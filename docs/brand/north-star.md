# Our North Star

# **We give a damn.**

Significa exists to prove that rigour and beauty aren't opposites.

We work on complex problems — enterprise systems, e-commerce at scale, tools that affect thousands of users — and we refuse to let complexity be an excuse for mediocrity. We believe that the harder the problem, the more it deserves thoughtful design.

We're not a software house. We're not a creative agency playing at tech. We're a team of designers and engineers who think together, build together, and hold each other to a standard most agencies gave up on.

Whether we're working with a global retailer or a non-profit changing how people access healthcare, the approach is the same: understand deeply, design with purpose, build with care. This means recommending what’s actually best for the client – even when a different answer would be easier for us.

### What This Means in Practice

Every project — regardless of client size or budget — should pass through these filters:

| Keyword | What it means internally | What it means for clients |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Purpose** | Every project needs a "why" beyond the spec. We need to understand and believe in what we're building. | We solve real problems for real users. |
| **Culture** | We're not pixel pushers or code monkeys. We question, we push back, we care — and we can explain why. Our opinions aren't arbitrary; they're grounded in experience and reasoning. | We're partners, not vendors. We bring perspective, not just execution. |
| **Art** | Not decoration. The obsession with how things feel, not just how they work. The trained eye that sees what others miss. The refusal to ship something you wouldn't be proud of. The chocolate on the side. | The output is noticeably better than alternatives. The output is noticeably better than what was expected. The cherry on top.  |

If it fails some, we need to be honest about what we're trading and why.

## Who We Are

### We are designers and engineers who think together.

Not a design agency that outsources dev. Not a dev shop with a "UI team." We're a single team where design and engineering are equal partners in solving problems. This also means decisions and responsibility live closer to the work itself, not in layers designed to mediate it.

### We back our opinions with reasoning and work.

Everyone has a voice — internally and with clients. We question, we push back, we bring perspective. But opinions alone aren't enough. We back them up with solid reasoning and great work. That's how trust is built and maintained, not by staying quiet until we've "earned" the right to speak.

### We care deeply but we ship.

We have high standards, but we're not precious. We don't hide behind perfectionism. We find the highest-quality version of what's possible within real constraints — and then we deliver it.

### We are calm in complexity.

Big projects, difficult integrations, legacy systems, corporate stakeholders — none of this makes us panic. We've done it. We're good at it. We bring clarity to chaos.

### We optimise for getting things done, not for documenting that we're doing things.

Process should serve the work, not the other way around. We've learned the hard way that elaborate systems often create more friction than clarity — but we've also learned that the right structure protects us, especially on complex projects with demanding clients. We default to the simplest process that works, and we adapt when the situation demands more rigour.

### We have structure without rigidity.

Roles and leadership exist because people need direction, accountability, and someone to make the call when consensus fails. But hierarchy doesn't mean blind agreement. A junior developer with a better idea should say so — and be heard. Partners roll up their sleeves on hard projects. The org chart describes responsibilities, not worth. We want the clarity of structure without the slowness of bureaucracy or the ego of rank.

### Freedom comes with accountability.

Freedom without accountability isn't freedom — it's unfairness to everyone else. When someone consistently doesn't hold up their end, we address it directly. Letting it slide isn't kindness; it's a disservice to the people picking up the slack.

### We tell each other the truth.

Honest feedback is how we get better — as individuals and as a team. We don't avoid hard conversations or let issues fester because they're uncomfortable.

Feedback should be direct, specific, and focused on the work. The goal is growth, not comfort — but also not venting. Being honest doesn't mean being harsh; we can be direct without being an asshole. And good feedback isn't just about *what* we say — it's knowing *when*, *where*, and *how*, because the same message lands differently depending on context and who's hearing it. We're not optimising for feelings; we're optimising for impact.

A safe environment and direct communication aren't opposites. You need both for people to take real risks and hear hard truths.

### We stay financially disciplined.

We're conservative with costs, careful with commitments, and we don't grow for growth's sake. This is what lets us say no to bad-fit work, weather slow periods without panic, and make decisions based on what's right rather than what's urgent.

### We aim to deliver both speed and quality.

This is often framed as a trade-off. We don't see it that way. With tight collaboration between design and engineering, clear scope from the start, and years of refining our tools and processes, we believe we can move fast without cutting corners. That's the bar we set for ourselves.

### We use AI to move faster, not to move cheaper.

AI accelerates execution, handles repetitive tasks, lets us explore more options faster. But it can generate a hundred variations without knowing which one is right for this brand, this user, this moment. That judgement is what we've been building for years. The time AI saves us goes into craft and thinking, not into squeezing margins.

### **We adapt to the moment.**

Not every project will be a portfolio piece. Not every client will want our opinion. Not every week allows for exploration. We know the difference between "this is who we are" and "this is what this moment requires."
Clients come to us mid-crisis, mid-pivot, mid-budget-cut. They have bosses, boards, and pressures we don't always see. Understanding their context — not just their brief — is part of our job. We can hold our standards while still meeting people where they are.
Principles guide us, but rigidity kills companies. We stay grounded in our values while responding to reality as it is — not as we wish it were.
Adapting to the moment never means silently absorbing unsustainable pressure, when reality consistently conflicts with our principles, that’s a signal to escalate, not endure.
