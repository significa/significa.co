Sometimes you arrive at a session and your mind goes blank. Or the conversation stalls and you're not sure where to take it next. That's what this page is for.

The questions here aren't interview questions or assessment checklists. They're prompts — invitations to think out loud, explore something you haven't looked at closely, or surface something that's been sitting below the surface.

You don't need to work through them in order, and you certainly don't need to ask all of them. Pick one, sit with it, and see where it goes.

---

## Getting started

For early sessions, when you're still building rapport and figuring out how you work together.

- What brought you to this field in the first place?
- What does a great week at work look like for you?
- What's something you've figured out in your career that took you longer than it should have?
- What do you most want to get out of our PAL sessions?
- What kind of support do you find most useful — someone who challenges you, someone who listens, or something else entirely?
- Is there anything you'd want me to know about how you work best?

## On growth and development

- What's the skill you're most actively working on right now?
- Where do you feel like you've grown the most in the last six months?
- What's an area where you know you need to improve, but keep avoiding?
- If you could go back and give yourself advice at the start of your career, what would you say?
- What does the next level look like for you — in concrete, observable terms?
- Is there something you want to be doing that you're not being given the opportunity to do yet?
- What's holding you back more — external circumstances, or something internal?

---

## On work and performance

- What kind of work are you doing when you feel most in flow?
- What's the most challenging project or task you've taken on recently? What made it hard?
- Where do you feel like you're delivering your best work right now?
- Where do you feel like you're underperforming, and why?
- What's the one thing that, if you improved it, would have the biggest impact on your work?
- How do you approach something when you're totally stuck?
- When was the last time you felt genuinely proud of something you made or contributed to?

---

## On collaboration and relationships

- How would you describe the way you work with others on your team?
- Is there a relationship at work — with a colleague, manager, or client — that you find challenging? What makes it hard?
- How comfortable are you asking for help when you need it?
- Are there moments where you feel underheard or overlooked? What's happening in those situations?
- How do you handle disagreement with someone you work closely with?
- What kind of environment brings out the best in you professionally?

---

## On feedback

- When did you last receive feedback that genuinely changed how you work?
- Is there feedback you've been given that you're still not sure what to do with?
- Who in your life gives you the most useful feedback, and what makes it land?
- Is there feedback you've been wanting to give someone but haven't? What's stopped you?
- How do you tend to react when feedback is critical? What does your internal response look like?
- What would need to be true for you to feel safe asking for honest feedback from anyone on the team?

---

## On motivation and energy

- What's energising you most at work right now?
- What's draining you?
- Are you feeling challenged enough, too much, or not enough?
- When was the last time you felt genuinely excited about coming to work? What was different then?
- What would need to change for you to feel more engaged or motivated?
- Is there something at work you've been putting off that's weighing on you?

---

## On career and the bigger picture

- Where do you want to be in two years — professionally and personally?
- Is the path you're on right now moving you in the direction you actually want to go?
- What kind of work do you want to be doing in five years that you're not doing today?
- Are there opportunities at Significa you haven't yet made use of? What's been in the way?
- What would a version of your career you're genuinely proud of look like?
- Is there a role model — someone whose career or way of working you admire? What is it about them?

---

## Going deeper

For sessions where you want to get into harder territory.

- What's the story you tell yourself about why something isn't working — and how much do you believe it?
- What's a belief you hold about yourself professionally that might be limiting you?
- Where are you playing it safe when you could be taking a more meaningful risk?
- What would you do differently if you weren't afraid of failing?
- Is there something you've been meaning to address or change for a long time but keep postponing?
- What do you think the people who know your work best would say is your biggest untapped potential?

---

## For mentors reflecting on their own practice

PAL is a two-way relationship. These questions are for the mentor to sit with.

- Am I asking enough questions, or am I talking too much?
- Am I giving my mentee room to find their own answers, or am I jumping in with mine?
- Is there something my mentee seems to avoid that I haven't yet found a way to address?
- What am I learning from this relationship?
- Am I showing up to these sessions as consistently and intentionally as I'd want my mentee to?
- Is there something I could share from my own experience that I've been holding back?
