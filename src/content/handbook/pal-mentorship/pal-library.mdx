The PAL library is a living collection of resources to help you and your PAL partner go deeper. Think of it as a shared shelf — something to draw from when you want to bring new ideas into your sessions, explore a topic together, or simply find inspiration when things feel a bit stuck.

You don't need to work through everything here. Pick what's relevant to where you are right now.

## On mentorship and coaching

**The Coaching Habit** — Michael Bungay Stanier
A short, practical book about asking better questions. The core idea: talk less, ask more. Essential reading for anyone in a mentoring role who finds themselves jumping to answers too quickly.

**The Feedback Fix** — Joe Hirsch
A reframe of how feedback works — moving away from retrospective critique and towards forward-looking, growth-oriented conversations. Particularly useful for the feedback dimension of PAL sessions.

**Radical Candor** — Kim Scott
The classic text on caring personally while challenging directly. Useful for understanding why honest feedback is an act of respect, not confrontation.

## On growth and self-awareness

**Mindset** — Carol Dweck
The foundational text on fixed vs. growth mindset. A reminder that how we think about our own abilities shapes whether we actually develop them.

**The Artist's Way** — Julia Cameron
Less about work, more about unlocking creative confidence and unblocking yourself. Recommended especially for people feeling stuck or uninspired.

**Designing Your Life** — Bill Burnett & Dave Evans
A Stanford-born framework for thinking about career and life through prototyping and curiosity rather than pressure and planning. Great to explore with your PAL when bigger career questions come up.

## On communication and collaboration

**Nonviolent Communication** — Marshall B. Rosenberg
A framework for expressing needs and hearing others without blame or judgement. Directly applicable to feedback conversations and moments of tension.

**Crucial Conversations** — Kerry Patterson et al.
Tools for navigating high-stakes conversations well. Useful when your PAL sessions touch on difficult topics — performance, conflict, uncertainty.

## Videos and talks

**"Every kid needs a champion"** — Rita Pierson (TED)
A short, energising talk about the power of believing in someone. A good reset when mentorship starts to feel transactional.

**"The power of vulnerability"** — Brené Brown (TED)
On why openness and imperfection are strengths, not weaknesses. Relevant to building the kind of trust that makes PAL sessions genuinely useful.

**"How to speak so that people want to listen"** — Julian Treasure (TED)
Practical techniques for more effective communication — relevant both in sessions and in wider professional life.

## Tools and frameworks

**The GROW model**
A classic coaching structure: Goal → Reality → Options → Will. Useful as a loose scaffold for sessions where your PAL partner is working through a specific challenge or decision.

**Johari Window**
A simple model for understanding the relationship between self-awareness and how others see us. Good to reference when exploring blind spots.

**The ABCD framework**
Significa's own performance model — Accountability, Business, Culture, Deliverables. Using it as a lens in PAL sessions helps connect personal development directly to how growth is recognised here. See Using ABCD to mentor for a full guide.

## Contributing to the library
